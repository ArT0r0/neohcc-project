About Our Project

The NeoHCC project is an international collaboration studying neoadjuvant immunotherapy for the treatment of hepatocellular carcinoma. The NeoHCC academic consortium is made of leading institutions in the field of liver cancer research, including Imperial College London, Mount Sinai, Johns Hopkins, and Taiwan National University (Our Team). Our goal is to improve treatment outcomes for patients through developing biological and digital biomarkers that can tailor the treatment selection for the patients.

If you are interested in collaborating with us, please refer to our Collaborator Portal for more information.

Research Objectives

  • Understand the biological correlates and determinants of response to immunotherapy
  • Identify predictive biomarkers of response, recurrence and survival outcomes
  • Understand the immune mechanisms that characterise HCC
  • Develop personalised treatment approaches

Current Focus

  • Biospecimen collection and analysis
  • Using bulk RNA sequencing to explore the immune mechanisms that determine response rates
  • Using Artificial Intelligence to evaluate and predict pathologic response to immunotherapy
  • Expanding our academic consortium
